Community organizations have long played a crucial role in bridging gaps left by traditional schooling systems.  
This brief examines three high-impact community-based models:

## 1. Public Library Workshops
Local libraries offering free, drop-in financial literacy sessions.

## 2. Neighborhood Mentorship Networks
Pairing financially skilled volunteers with local youth.

## 3. Small Business Partnerships
Leveraging local businesses as teaching hubs for budgeting and entrepreneurship.

## Conclusion
Community-driven models foster trust and engagement, making them a vital part of any state-level financial literacy strategy.
